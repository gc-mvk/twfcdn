Sa'y Worship: Safa and Marwa

A short educational page about walking between Safa and Marwa during Hajj and Umrah.

The Sa'y area between Safa and Marwa inside Masjid al-Haram.

What Is Sa'y?

Sa'y is the act of walking between the hills of Safa and Marwa as part of Hajj and Umrah. Pilgrims complete seven passages, beginning at Safa and ending at Marwa.

This worship is connected to the story of Hajar, peace be upon her, who searched with trust and patience for water for her son Prophet Ismail, peace be upon him. Her effort is remembered by Muslims as a symbol of faith, perseverance, and reliance on Allah.

Spiritual Meaning

Sa'y teaches that sincere effort and trust in Allah belong together. A pilgrim walks with remembrance, dua, and reflection, remembering that Allah sees every struggle and every step taken for His sake.

The movement between Safa and Marwa also reminds believers that worship is not only stillness and prayer, but can also include patient action, hope, and determination.

Good Manners During Sa'y

Pilgrims should perform Sa'y with calmness, cleanliness, and respect for others. Because the area can be crowded, it is important not to push, block pathways, or disturb other worshippers.

For detailed rulings about Sa'y, including special situations for elderly, sick, or wheelchair users, pilgrims should follow official Hajj and Umrah guidance or consult qualified scholars.
