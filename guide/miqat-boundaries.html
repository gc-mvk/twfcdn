Miqat Boundaries

A short educational page about the sacred entry points for Hajj and Umrah pilgrims.

Masjid al-Haram in Makkah, toward which pilgrims travel after entering ihram at the Miqat.

What Is a Miqat?

Miqat refers to the appointed boundary where a pilgrim intending Hajj or Umrah enters the sacred state of ihram before proceeding toward Makkah.

These boundaries are important because they remind pilgrims that the journey is not only physical, but also spiritual. Before crossing the Miqat, a pilgrim prepares with intention, humility, and respect for the sacred rites.

Main Miqat Locations

The Miqat points are located around Makkah in different directions, serving pilgrims who arrive from various regions. Some of the well-known Miqat locations include Dhul Hulayfah, Al-Juhfah, Qarn al-Manazil, Yalamlam, and Dhat Irq.

  • Dhul Hulayfah: Commonly used by pilgrims coming from Madinah.
  • Al-Juhfah: Used by pilgrims coming from the direction of the Levant and nearby routes.
  • Qarn al-Manazil: Used by pilgrims coming from the direction of Najd and eastern routes.
  • Yalamlam: Used by pilgrims coming from the direction of Yemen and southern routes.
  • Dhat Irq: Used by pilgrims coming from the direction of Iraq and northeastern routes.

Why the Miqat Matters

Crossing the Miqat without proper preparation is a serious matter in pilgrimage guidance. For this reason, pilgrims usually learn their route in advance and make sure they enter ihram before passing the correct boundary.

Travelers arriving by plane are often advised to prepare before reaching the Miqat area, because the aircraft may pass over or near the boundary before landing. Pilgrims should follow reliable guidance from their Hajj or Umrah group, official authorities, or qualified scholars.
