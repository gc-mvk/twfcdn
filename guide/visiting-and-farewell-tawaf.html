Visiting and Farewell Tawaf

A short educational page about two important Tawaf rites connected with Hajj.

Pilgrims performing Tawaf around the Kaaba in Masjid al-Haram.

What Is Visiting Tawaf?

Visiting Tawaf, also known as Tawaf az-Ziyarah or Tawaf al-Ifadah, is one of the major rites of Hajj. It is performed after key rites such as standing at Arafat and spending time in Muzdalifah.

This Tawaf represents a return to the Kaaba after the great gathering at Arafat. It is a moment of worship, gratitude, and renewed devotion to Allah.

What Is Farewell Tawaf?

Farewell Tawaf, known as Tawaf al-Wada, is performed before leaving Makkah at the end of Hajj. It is a final act of worship around the Kaaba before the pilgrim departs from the sacred city.

This farewell is often an emotional moment. Pilgrims remember the blessings of their journey, ask Allah for acceptance, and hope to carry the lessons of Hajj into their daily lives.

Spiritual Lessons

Both Tawaf az-Ziyarah and Tawaf al-Wada remind pilgrims that Hajj is a journey of return: returning to Allah, returning to sincerity, and returning to a life guided by worship and good character.

For exact rulings about timing, obligation, exemptions, and special circumstances, pilgrims should follow official Hajj guidance or consult qualified scholars.
