Visiting the Rawda-i Mutahhara

A short educational page about visiting the blessed Rawda in Al-Masjid an-Nabawi.

Al-Masjid an-Nabawi in Madinah, where the Rawda-i Mutahhara is located.

What Is the Rawda-i Mutahhara?

The Rawda-i Mutahhara, also known as the Noble Rawda, is a blessed area inside the Prophet's Mosque in Madinah. It is located between the house of Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, and his pulpit.

This place holds a very special spiritual value for Muslims. Visitors come with love, humility, and respect, remembering the Prophet's mercy, guidance, and lifelong devotion to calling people to Allah.

Etiquette During the Visit

A visit to the Rawda should be made calmly and respectfully. Because many people wish to pray there, visitors should avoid pushing, raising their voices, or disturbing others. Patience and good manners are part of the beauty of the visit.

  • Enter with humility and remembrance of Allah.
  • Keep your voice low and your behavior gentle.
  • Pray if space and time are available.
  • Make sincere dua without causing difficulty for others.

A Moment of Reflection

Visiting the Rawda-i Mutahhara is not only a physical visit, but also a reminder to follow the Prophet's noble character. It encourages believers to increase prayer, kindness, patience, and love for the Sunnah.
