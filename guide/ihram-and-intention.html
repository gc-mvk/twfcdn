Ihram and Intention

A short educational page about entering the sacred state for Hajj or Umrah.

Pilgrims in ihram garments during worship near Masjid al-Haram.

What Is Ihram?

Ihram is the sacred state a pilgrim enters before performing Hajj or Umrah. It is not only about wearing special garments; it also means beginning the journey of worship with humility, discipline, and awareness of Allah.

For men, ihram is commonly represented by two simple white cloths. Women wear modest clothing that fulfills Islamic guidelines. The simplicity of ihram reminds pilgrims that all people stand before Allah without pride, luxury, or worldly status.

The Importance of Intention

The intention, known as niyyah, is made in the heart before beginning the rites. A pilgrim intends to perform Hajj, Umrah, or both, depending on the type of pilgrimage being undertaken.

After making the intention, pilgrims commonly recite the Talbiyah, declaring their response to the call of Allah. This marks the beginning of a special spiritual journey filled with remembrance, patience, and devotion.

Respecting the Sacred State

While in ihram, pilgrims avoid certain actions and focus on worship, good manners, and self-control. The goal is to purify the heart and prepare for the sacred rites of Hajj or Umrah.

For detailed rulings about ihram and intention, pilgrims should consult qualified scholars or official Hajj and Umrah guidance from trusted religious authorities.
