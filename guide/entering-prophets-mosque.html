Entering the Prophet's Mosque

A respectful educational page about entering Al-Masjid an-Nabawi in Madinah.

An entrance area of the Prophet's Mosque in Madinah.

The Sacredness of the Place

The Prophet's Mosque, known as Al-Masjid an-Nabawi, is one of the most honored mosques in Islam. It is located in Madinah, the city to which Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, migrated and where the Muslim community grew.

Entering this mosque is a moment of humility, remembrance, and gratitude. Visitors are encouraged to enter calmly, maintain cleanliness, lower their voices, and remember that they are stepping into a place of worship and deep spiritual respect.

Recommended Etiquette

A Muslim generally enters the mosque with the right foot first and says the appropriate supplication for entering a mosque. The visitor should avoid pushing, disturbing others, taking unnecessary space, or treating the visit like ordinary sightseeing.

  • Enter with calmness and respect.
  • Keep the mosque clean and peaceful.
  • Pray two units of greeting prayer if it is an appropriate time.
  • Send blessings upon the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him.

A Place of Prayer and Reflection

The Prophet's Mosque reminds believers of faith, mercy, brotherhood, and devotion to Allah. A visit should inspire better character, stronger worship, and love for the Prophet's guidance.
