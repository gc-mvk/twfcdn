Greeting the Black Stone

A short educational page about the respectful greeting of Hajar al-Aswad during Tawaf.

Hajar al-Aswad, the Black Stone, is located at one corner of the Kaaba.

What Is the Black Stone?

The Black Stone, known in Arabic as Hajar al-Aswad, is located in the eastern corner of the Kaaba. It marks the starting point of each circuit of Tawaf.

Muslims respect the Black Stone because of its connection to the rites of pilgrimage. They do not worship the stone; worship belongs to Allah alone.

How Pilgrims Greet It

During Tawaf, pilgrims begin each circuit from the direction of the Black Stone. When possible and without harming others, some pilgrims touch or kiss it as part of the pilgrimage practice.

If the area is crowded, pilgrims may simply face or gesture toward the Black Stone from a distance while continuing their Tawaf. Safety, patience, and respect for other worshippers are very important.

Spiritual Reminder

Greeting the Black Stone reminds pilgrims of obedience, humility, and following the example taught in the pilgrimage rites. The focus should remain on remembering Allah and completing Tawaf with sincerity.

Pilgrims should follow official guidance and consult qualified scholars or trusted Hajj and Umrah guides for detailed rulings related to touching, kissing, or gesturing toward the Black Stone.
