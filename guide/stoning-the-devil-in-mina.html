Stoning the Devil in Mina

A short educational page about the Jamarat rite performed during Hajj.

The Jamarat area in Mina, where pilgrims perform the symbolic stoning rite during Hajj.

What Is the Stoning Rite?

Stoning the Devil in Mina, also known as Ramy al-Jamarat, is one of the important rites of Hajj. Pilgrims throw small pebbles at the Jamarat pillars in Mina as a symbolic act of rejecting evil and disobedience to Allah.

The rite is connected to the legacy of Prophet Ibrahim, peace be upon him, and his firm obedience to Allah. It reminds pilgrims to resist temptation, strengthen faith, and choose submission over selfish desire.

Spiritual Meaning

The pebbles are small, but the meaning is great. Each throw can remind the pilgrim to reject arrogance, anger, greed, envy, and every path that leads away from Allah.

This worship teaches that the believer's struggle against evil is not only outside, but also inside the heart. Hajj trains the pilgrim to return home with stronger discipline, sincerity, and awareness.

Safety and Good Manners

Because many pilgrims gather in Mina, safety and patience are very important. Pilgrims should follow official crowd-control instructions, avoid rushing, and never push or harm others.

For exact rulings about timing, number of pebbles, order of the Jamarat, and special situations, pilgrims should follow official Hajj guidance or consult qualified scholars.
