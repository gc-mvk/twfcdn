Standing at Muzdalifah

A short educational page about the Hajj stay at Muzdalifah after Arafat.

Muzdalifah is a sacred area where pilgrims stay after leaving Arafat during Hajj.

What Is Muzdalifah?

Muzdalifah is a sacred area between Arafat and Mina. During Hajj, pilgrims travel there after standing at Arafat on the 9th day of Dhul Hijjah.

The stay at Muzdalifah is a time of calmness, remembrance, and preparation for the next stage of Hajj. Pilgrims spend part of the night there, remembering Allah and resting after the long day at Arafat.

Spiritual Meaning

Standing or staying at Muzdalifah reminds pilgrims that worship includes patience, simplicity, and trust in Allah. The open plain, the night sky, and the gathering of pilgrims create a powerful reminder of humility before Allah.

Many pilgrims use this time for prayer, dua, and reflection. It is also commonly the place where pilgrims collect small pebbles for the rite of stoning at the Jamarat in Mina.

Good Conduct at Muzdalifah

Pilgrims should remain patient, avoid arguments, and respect the space of others. Since many people gather in one area, cleanliness, calm movement, and cooperation are very important.

For exact rulings about the timing, prayers, overnight stay, and special situations at Muzdalifah, pilgrims should follow official Hajj guidance or consult qualified scholars.
